Available Plants in Jordan

Olive Tree

The olive tree is one of the most iconic and ancient trees in Jordan, deeply connected to the culture and daily life of its people. It grows abundantly in the northern and central regions, where the climate and soil are ideal for it's cultivation. The olive fruit is harvested every autumn and is used both for direct consumption and for producing olive oil, which is a cornerstone of Jordanian cuisine. Olive trees are extremely common and can be found in farms, gardens, and even around villages.

Fig

The fig is a traditional and beloved fruit in Jordan, known for its natural sweetness and rich flavor. It ripens in the summer months, when it is eaten fresh, and it is also dried to be enjoyed throughout the year. Figs are widely available across villages, gardens, and small farms. They have been part of Jordanian diets for centuries, making them a familiar and very common fruit.

Almond

The almond is a popular and nutritious nut in Jordan, appreciated for its flavor and health benefits. Almonds are harvested in late summer, and they are often used in traditional sweets, desserts, and festive dishes. While the almond tree is common in several regions, the nut itself is the main product recognized and consumed. Almonds are well known throughout the country and are widely enjoyed.

Carob Tree

The carob tree is an evergreen tree that thrives in the Mediterranean climate of Jordan. It's dark brown pods are used in a variety of ways: ground into powder, made into syrups, or used as a natural sweetener. Carob has both nutritional and medicinal value, and it also serves as fodder for animals. While not as widespread as the olive or fig, carob trees are still found in hilly and rural areas, making them a familiar though less dominant plant.

Basil

Basil is a fragrant herb that is widely grown in Jordanian households and gardens. It is used in cooking to add flavor to many traditional dishes, and it also has a role in herbal medicine due to it's health properties. Basil grows easily in pots or small garden spaces, which makes it one of the most common herbs to find around Jordanian homes. It's pleasant aroma and versatile uses make it a staple herb in daily life.

Mint

The national flower of Jordan, recognized by it's dark purple petals. It grows in rocky areas and is considered endangered. Mint is perhaps the most popular herb in Jordan, famous for its refreshing aroma and taste. It is commonly used to prepare tea, a traditional drink in almost every household, and it is also added to salads and cooked dishes. Mint grows very quickly and spreads easily, which is why almost every Jordanian garden or balcony has a patch of it. Because of this, mint is considered one of the most widespread and essential herbs in Jordan.
