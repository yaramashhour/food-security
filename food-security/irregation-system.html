Irrigation system

Sprinkler Irrigation

Sprinkler irrigation is a method of applying water to crops in the form of artificial rainfall. Water is distributed through pipes and sprayed into the air through sprinklers, breaking up into small droplets that fall on the plants and soil.

Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation delivers water directly to the roots of plants through small tubes or emitters. It saves water, reduces weed growth, and increases fertilizer efficiency, making it ideal for orchards and vegetable farms.

Surface Irrigation system

Surface irrigation is a traditional method where water flows across the field surface by gravity. It is easy to apply and low-cost, but less efficient and often leads to water loss and uneven distribution..

Subsurface Irrigation

Smart irrigation uses sensors and automated controllers to adjust watering based on soil moisture and weather conditions. It improves water efficiency, reduces waste, and supports sustainable farming.
