Available Crops in Jordan

Tomato

The tomato is one of the most widely cultivated vegetables in Jordan, grown in open fields and greenhouses throughout the year. It's a key ingredient in salads, sauces, and cooked dishes in Jordanian cuisine. Tomatoes are very common, and their production is continuous to meet local demand. The plants grow quickly, producing large yields, which makes them easily accessible in local markets.

Cucumber

The cucumber is a popular vegetable in Jordan, grown both in greenhouses and outdoor farms. It's mainly consumed fresh in salads, pickles, and sandwiches. Cucumbers are abundant and available almost year-round, making them a familiar and essential crop in the Jordanian diet. Their fast growth and adaptability contribute to their widespread cultivation.

Beans

Beans are an important legume crop in Jordan, especially in northern regions. They are used in traditional dishes such as stews and rice meals. Beans are grown seasonally and are considered a common crop, though production can vary depending on rainfall and growing conditions. Their nutritional value and versatility make them a staple in many households.

Potato

The potato is a widely grown crop in Jordan, particularly in the northern and central areas. It's a staple in daily meals and are used in many traditional dishes. Potatoes are highly versatile in cooking, and their cultivation is consistent, ensuring they remain widely available in local markets. They are considered an essential crop for food security.

Wheat

Wheat is one of the most important staple crops in Jordan, widely cultivated across the northern and central regions. It serves as the main ingredient for bread, pastries, and other traditional foods. Jordanian wheat varieties are well adapted to local climates, and the crop is abundant and central to the countryâ€™s agriculture. It's cultivation supports both local consumption and economic stability in rural areas.

Barley

Barley is a resilient cereal crop that thrives even in semi-arid conditions. In Jordan, it is grown both as a food source for humans and as animal feed. It's adaptability to poor soils and dry weather makes barley a common crop, particularly in regions where wheat may not grow as well. Barley is widely recognized and regularly harvested across many parts of the country.






Rare Crops in Jordan

Saffron

Saffron is a highly valued spice grown only in very limited mountainous areas of Jordan. The red stigmas of the saffron flower are harvested by hand, making the process labor-intensive and the spice rare and expensive. Saffron is prized for it's culinary uses, vibrant color, aroma, and medicinal properties. It's limited cultivation adds to it's rarity and uniqueness in Jordanian agriculture.

Black Pomegranate

The black pomegranate is a rare and unique variety found in specific regions. It's dark red seeds are highly flavorful and sought after for fresh consumption and traditional recipes. This variety is not commonly grown, and it's limited availability makes it a specialty crop with cultural significance.

Pineapple

Pineapple is not a traditional Jordanian crop and is cultivated mainly in greenhouses or controlled environments. It's tropical nature means it requires specific growing conditions, making it rare and less common in local markets. Pineapple is mostly produced for niche consumption and is valued for it's sweet taste.

Lavender

Lavender is an aromatic plant grown in small-scale farms primarily for it's flowers and essential oils. While it is intentionally cultivated, it is not widespread and is considered a rare crop. Lavender is used in perfumes, teas, culinary dishes, and as an ornamental plant, making it valuable both commercially and aesthetically.

Quinoa

Quinoa is a relatively new crop in Jordan, introduced for health-focused farming and experimental cultivation. It grows in select areas and is valued for it's high protein content and nutritional benefits. It's cultivation is limited, which makes it rare compared to more traditional grains like wheat and barley.

Traditional/Old Wheat

Traditional or old wheat varieties were commonly grown in Jordan before modern high-yield wheat replaced them. These varieties are now rare but are valued for their unique flavor, nutritional content, and historical importance. Farmers cultivating old wheat aim to preserve agricultural heritage, and the crop is still appreciated for special bread-making and cultural dishes.
